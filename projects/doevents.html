CONTROL


The simulation below is the ice cover in the control simulation between years 3300 and 3600. The black lines in the plots on the right show the North Atlantic (defined as (-80 .lt. tlon .lt. 60) and (0 .lt. tlat .lt. 80)) sea ice area of control. Red is a 15 year running mean. The lower plot shows the nearest neighbor surface temp. in NGRIP. Grey curves are for the perturbation experiment, purple is 15 year running mean.
The control simulations has a rapid sea ice increase around year 100 which is not seen in the Southern Ocean perturbation experiment. Thus, between year 175 and 250, the sea ice cover in the EXP_SO is only 60% of that in the control simulation.

EXP_SO


If we now look at the spatial extent of the sea ice in EXP_SO, the differences are clearly strongest in the SPG and the Nordic Seas. However, it appears the sea ice is not removed by the SO perturbation, rather, it inhibits the increase in sea ice cover seen in CONTROL. Note that the colors in the right hand side plots are now switched to highlight which simulation is shown on the left.

EXP_SPG


Below follows the same figure, but for the SPG experiment (black and red) and the control (grey and purple). The sea ice in the Labrador Sea develops as in the control simulation, but the Nordic Seas remain largely ice free, as Mehmet also showed. NGRIP temperatures are only slightly warmer than in the control simulation.
